Green Goblin

Norman Osborn, also well known as the Green Goblin, along with other known ailas, is one of the main major antagonists from Marvel Comics. He is also the main anotagonist of the main spiderman comic book series.

Peter Parker was a timid fifteen-year-old Midtown High School student who was bitten by a radioactive spider, which gave him superhuman spider based-powers and abilities, such as; superhuman strength, speed, agility, leaping, reflexes, stamina, durability, coordination and balance, a healing-factor, immunity, the ability to wall-crawl, and sensing/detecting any kind threats and upcoming danger known as "Spider-sense". He also develops his wrist-mounted high speeding device equipment that resembles spider-webbing on each of his wrists known as "web-shooters" as his main primary weapon, which enables him to web-shoot at his enemies like a web-bullets, and web-swing across the city at high speeds. Peter originally intended to utilize his newly found abilities to help provide for his Uncle Ben Parker and Aunt May Parker, Peter allowed his fame to get over his head until the death of his Uncle Ben, who was murdered by the thief whom unkbenkownsit to Peter let escape. After his death, Peter vowed to use his spider-powers to fight crime and protect the people of New York City from the evil forces as masked vigilante superhero; Spider-Man, having learned the most valuable lesson "With great power, there must also come great responsibility". Spider-Man has gained friends and has battled his main enemies such as his arch-enemy, Green Goblin, and then Doctor Octopus, Sandman, Chameleon, Vulture, Mysterio, Electro, Rhino, Shocker, Kingpin, Venom, Carnage, and many more but has also dealt with the ups and downs of life ever since his debut as a superhero.

Motivations:

Upon gaining his powers from getting bitten by a radioactive spider, Peter originally choses to use them for monitary gain by appering in late night live tv shows as a performer, in order to provide his Uncle Ben Parker and Aunt May Parker, casusing all the fame and attention to get over his head. This would ultimately change when Peter had allowed a theif to escape from robbing the studio he was at, inevitably leading to his uncle's murder. Through the guilt of the fact that he could have done something about it and the love for his Uncle Ben, cause him to adopt the qoute "With Great Power Comes Great Responsibility", chosing to now use his powers to protect the streets of New York City and fight crime. For Peter, each time he has saved lives and stoping criminals would be atoning for his mistake of letting his uncle dying, honoring his legacy and hopefully making him proud. Throughout his life, he has allways made the hard choices sacrificing most of his personal and professional life, putting the needs of his lovedones and others before himself while strugling to balance out his life as Peter Parker and Spider-Man.

Notable Crimes and Questionable Deeds Committed:

  • Vigilantism and Obstruction Of Justice

    Spider-Man reguarly fights crime while operating outside the law, which does make him technicly labeled as a vigilante. Although he has apprehended and detained various criminals and Supervillians under Citizen's Arrest, he still breaks certian laws while doing so. Spider-Man has also worked with and assoiated himself with Law Enforcement and other authorities to presue criminals.

  • Assult and Battery

    Spider-Man regualy physically assults criminals and Supervillians alike throughout his carrer as a superhero, although most of the cases are usallily commited in self-defense or in the defense of others.

  • Trespassing and Unlawful Entry

    In certian storylines, Spider-Man tresspasses into places and properties without any legal athority or even a permit. Although in most cases, the locations he breaks into belong to criminals and Supervillians, usallily under the suspistion of conspiracy.

  • Muliptle Acounts of Forgery and Fraud

    As Peter Parker, he has occationally sold photos of himself as his alter-ego, Spider-Man to the Daily Bugle as the company's Photographer. He has also manipulated photographs for varios reasons, depending on the situations usallily to protect his identity. Despite this, these are cases of fraud. During the Civil War Storyline, Spider-Man reviled his secrect identity to the world, prompting J. Jonah Jameson into firing him for fraud.

  • Weapons Manufacturing

    Throughout the years Spider-Man has invented and developed tools and gadgets to aid him in his carrer as a superhero, such as his iconic Web-Shooters. However, these can be classified as weapons, given the potential of casusing harm to others and potential damages.

  • Property Damage and Public Endangerment

    Spider-Man engages in fights with Supervillians, unwillingly damaging nearby property while also endangering innocent-by-standers in the process. Despite this, Spider-Man does attempt in reduce as much collateral damage as possilble.

  • Concealing Identity

    As a Vigilante, Spider-Man reguarly keeps his identity a secrect from everyone, which does include law enforcement, without much of legal athority to do so (although he does have certin rights to be anonymous). Despite this, certian organisations such as S.H.I.E.L.D. are aware of his secret identity.

  • Muliptle Acounts of Resisting Arrest

    In certain cases Spider-Man has resisted any arrest attempted by the NYPD. Although these arresting attempts usallily happens out of the occassion of him being framed, a misunderstanding, or even a issued bounty on his head.

  • Involuntary Manslaguters

    Throughout the years Spider-Man never intentionally kill anyone throughout his carrer as a superhero, however he has done so by accident and some of them haunted him for the rest of his life. In the story 'Spider-Man vs Wolverine' he was caught by surprise by Wolverine's partner, Chaleange, prominting him to strike a punch that was ment for Wolverine (given that he would survive it) but instead accidently killing her. Wolverine had no grudge about it but haunted Spider-Man for the rest of his life. In the infamous story, 'The Night Gwen Stacy Died', The Green Goblin dropped the unconscious body of Gwen Stacy (Peter Parker's first girlfriend) from the golden state brigde, casusing Spider-Man to attempt to catch her with his webbing, however the wiplash casued by both the fall and Spider-Man's web catching her, ultimately broke her neck, killing her. Although the Green Goblin is mostly responsible for this event, Spider-Man blames himself, veiwing it as him being reckless while still blaming the Green Goblin.

Justification of Actions:

The Majory of Spider-Man's actions are completely justified, given the greater good he accomplishes by stoping criminals and Supervillians alike as a Superhero. The Legal System of the Marvel Comics Universe completely, providing him with a degree of legal immunity and/or support.
